DANCE

I Love Dancing!

Each time I get to dance, it is a beautiful experience. An extravaganza of body synchronisation and expressions, it is so enjoyable. I love to take part in dance events, I reckon I am slightly above average; so usually am not told to stand exclusively as a background dancer. I like learning new hook steps(especially on Kartik Aaryan songs), and don't mind taking to the dance floor at parties:)

Here are some of my favourite dance forms:

Bollywood
Break Dance
Bhangra

Bollywood dance, a dazzling fusion of traditional and modern styles, mirrors the glitz of Indian cinema, featuring expressive gestures and vibrant choreography. I absolutely love trying to step into the shoes of bollywood superstars, also the expressions and lipsync makes this real fun.

Break dance, born from the streets, showcases athleticism and creativity through its explosive movements, embodying the dynamic essence of urban expression. Matching the energy of the beats, and learning pop-culture symbolic steps like helicopters, spongebobs and locking is challenging but super interesting!

Bhangra, with its infectious beats and spirited footwork, represents the exuberance of Punjabi culture, adding a joyful rhythm to celebrations. Being from Punjab, this is one I'm used to seeing at all my relatives' marriages and functions. The Dhol and Bhangra have synergy like no other dance, I guarrantee you! :)

Freshiezza 23-24

I participated in group dance in a Freshie dance event last sem. It was a blast. I do think we did pretty decent, event though we didn't win. Our mentors who were sophie seniors were so good, supportive and sweet. I believe My dance skills did improve, slightly at least, and really look forward to participating in AIDS !

Dance is not "Just Dance"

I do love the graceful swirling of the human body that makes dance a visual treat as well as a great physical activity, but there's another side to it. The new people we meet, the memories we make together, and all the fun we have in practice; is priceless. Each time have participated in some event, I have got to meet great mentors, and made at least a couple of new friends too. Those bonds, and the jokes we made together, stay with me forever. An introverted ambivert making friends is really complicated, but dance really does simplify it!
