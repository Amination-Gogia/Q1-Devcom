READING

When I'm going through a burnout, books become my best friends. Sumptuous texts by story-telling experts enchant me and rid me of my boredom. Fantasy gives my imagination wings, while thrillers manage to keep me hooked till the very end.

As I've grown up, various books and genres have fascinated me. Here are some of my previously favourite ones:

The orange furred rat editor was my favourite protagonist as a 5th grade kid. His exciting adventures were described brilliantly along with visually captivating images, and subtle humour. Loved the stories of time-travel and The Kingdom of Fantasy.

Charlie, Matilda, the BFG, Wonka: his lovely characters would win one's heart. With Oompa-Loompas and giants, Roald Dahl had created an entire universe(and a dictionary) of his. The junior-school me would never complain of no images while reading them.

An all time classic series. Middle school me was mad about this, I would re-read these books endlessly. The way JK Rowling made Harry Potter a hearthrob is truly "Wizardry". Larger than life personalities in the awe-inspiring Wizarding multiverse are just the icing on the cake.

My Current Favourite: Murder Mysteries

A crime scene. Unorthodox criminals, their peculiar crimes, their expertise at leaving seemingly no evidence. And then some ingenious investigative work to solve the case. Cliche' as it may be, I can't get enough of this genre' right now. The suspense of the stories, further emphasised by great imagery of serious crime scene situations fails to disappoint.
